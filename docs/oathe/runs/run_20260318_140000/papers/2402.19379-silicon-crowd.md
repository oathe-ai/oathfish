# Wisdom of the Silicon Crowd: LLM Ensemble Prediction Capabilities Rival Human Crowd Accuracy

**arxiv**: 2402.19379
**Perspective**: ENSEMBLE_FORECASTING

## Abstract

Human forecasting accuracy relies on the 'wisdom of the crowd' effect, where predictions are significantly improved by aggregating across individual forecasters. Past work suggests frontier LLMs, as individual forecasters, underperform compared to human crowd aggregates. Study 1 uses an LLM ensemble of 12 models, comparing aggregated predictions on 31 binary questions to 925 human forecasters from a three-month tournament. The LLM crowd outperforms a 50% benchmark and is not statistically different from the human crowd. Study 2 tests whether LLM predictions improve from exposure to human cognitive output, finding 17-28% accuracy improvement — though this leads to less accurate predictions than simply averaging human and machine forecasts.

## Key Findings (with statistics)

### Study 1: Ensemble vs Human Crowd
- **LLM crowd Brier score**: M=0.20 (SD=0.12)
- **Human crowd Brier score**: M=0.19 (SD=0.19)
- **Not statistically different**: t(60) = 0.19, p = 0.850
- **Equivalence confirmed** with medium effect size bounds (Cohen's d=0.5)
- **Best individual model**: GPT-4 at 0.15
- **Worst model**: Coral (Command) at 0.38
- **Human crowd**: 0.19
- **50% baseline**: 0.25

### Acquiescence Bias
- Mean model predictions significantly above 50%: M=57.35, t(1006)=86.20, p<0.001
- Despite near-even split of positive/negative resolutions (14/31 positive)
- LLMs favor positive predictions above empirical expectation

### Calibration
- **Poor calibration** across most individual models
- **Aggregate calibration index**: 0.041 (best)
- **Best individual**: Falcon-180B at 0.027
- **Worst**: Coral (Command) at 0.212
- Overconfidence in aggregate: models overpredict outcomes

### Study 2: Human Data Improves LLMs
- GPT-4 improved from 0.17 to 0.14 after seeing human median (p=0.003)
- Claude 2 improved from 0.22 to 0.15 (p<0.001)
- **BUT**: Simple average of human+machine predictions BEATS the LLM's own update
  - GPT-4: updated=0.14, simple avg=0.13, t(92)=2.583, p=0.011
  - Claude 2: updated=0.15, simple avg=0.14, t(92)=3.530, p=0.001
- Models update directionally correctly but FAIL to beat simple averaging

## OathFish Relevance

- **Validates ensemble approach**: 12 diverse models = human-level accuracy
- **CRITICAL WARNING**: Simple averaging beats deliberative updating. Does OathFish's 6-round deliberation ACTUALLY add value over just averaging 30 independent predictions?
- **Acquiescence bias**: Archetypes will inherit this. Need explicit counteraction (contrarian archetypes, adversarial debate)
- **Calibration is poor individually, better in aggregate**: Supports ensemble architecture
- **Must A/B test**: Run amplification BEFORE and AFTER deliberation to measure if deliberation adds or destroys value
