# Generative Agent Simulations of 1,000 People

**arxiv**: 2411.10109
**Authors**: Joon Sung Park et al. (Stanford University)
**Perspective**: PERSONA_FIDELITY

## Abstract

This paper presents an agent architecture that simulates the attitudes and behaviors of 1,052 real individuals by applying LLMs to qualitative interviews about their lives. Generative agents replicate participants' responses on the General Social Survey 85% as accurately as participants replicate their own answers two weeks later, and perform comparably in predicting personality traits and outcomes in experimental replications. The architecture reduces accuracy biases across racial and ideological groups compared to agents given demographic descriptions alone.

## Key Findings

1. **85% fidelity**: LLM agents replicate real individuals' survey responses 85% as accurately as participants replicate their OWN answers 2 weeks later
2. **Deep interviews > demographics**: Architecture based on qualitative interviews dramatically outperforms demographic-only persona descriptions
3. **Bias reduction**: Reduces accuracy biases across racial and ideological groups compared to demographic-only agents
4. **Two access tiers**: Open access for aggregated responses on fixed tasks (e.g., GSS), restricted access for individualized responses
5. **Real people as ground truth**: Each agent is grounded in actual interview transcripts from a real person

## Critical Methodology

- Used REAL qualitative interviews (not synthetic persona generation)
- Ground truth from actual survey responses
- 1,052 individuals — statistically meaningful sample
- Compared against demographic-only baseline (significantly worse)
- Measured test-retest reliability as the benchmark (85% of human self-consistency)
